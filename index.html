We are a neurodivergent led charity dedicated to improving the mental health of neurodivergent people across the UK.

We invite you to join us in nurturing a society in which Every Mind Is Valued.

donate get help now

In a system that is already stretched, neurodivergent people are facing a mental health crisis:

79% of autistic adults have been affected by mental health problems

Adults with ADHD are 5 times more likely to die by suicide than the general population

Dyslexic young people are more likely to experience poor self-esteem, anxiety and depression

Adults with tourette's syndrome are 4 times more likely to die by suicide than the general population

Autistic adults are 7.55 times more likely to die by suicide than the general population

If you are diagnosed with ADHD in childhood, you are at a greater risk of a wide range of mental health problems by the end of adolescence

Children with DCD are at a higher risk of developing anxiety and depression

30% of people with Tourette's Syndrome have co-occurring anxiety and/ or depression

42% of autistic children are affected by anxiety disorders, as compared to 3% of non-autistic children

Adults with adhd are 4.5 times more likely to have a major depressive disorder and/ or substance use disorder than than the general population

Young people with reading difficulties are at a greater risk of suicidal ideation and suicide attempts

1 in 10 children with Tourette's Syndrome or Chronic Tic Disorder will think about, or attempt, suicide
