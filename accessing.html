Accessing a GP

If you are struggling with your mental health and would like to find support, seeing your GP can be an important first step.

Your GP can provide you with advice and options for care and treatment. Your GP may also refer you to more specialist services for additional support.

Visiting a GP can feel overwhelming or difficult for many reasons - particularly when you are neurodivergent. Challenges we may face include:

The sensory environment – i.e. the lighting, sounds or smells can makes us feel uncomfortable.

The waiting room – waiting in a busy, unfamiliar environment can cause a lot of anxiety.

Making an appointment.

Getting to the appointment on time.

Not knowing what to expect.

Finding it hard to express our feelings or experiences.

Past experiences of not being listened to.

Concerns about being judged.

Feeling that health professionals do not understand us.

Everyone should be equally able to access the support they need from their GP.

This is a legal duty. It should not have to be difficult for you. Neurodivergent MiNDs is here to support you in this. We have provided some information and tools below so you can access effective treatment and support.

Keeping a record of your feelings and symptoms can provide useful information for your GP.

A simple way to do this is to use a mood diary, or mood tracker. There are a number of templates and apps available for this. Use search terms such as ‘mood diary template’ or ‘mood tracker’ to find a format that suits you best.

Here are some useful resources we have found:

My Possible Self is a mental health app, approved by the NHS, which prompts you to track your mood each day. You can track how your mood changes over time to notice patterns that may be affecting your mood.

Leeds Autism AIM have produced resources to help you record and communicate your health needs. All resources were co-produced with autistic adults.

The Foundation for People with Learning Disabilities has produced some free Easy Read resources to support you to look after your mental health. These include resources for:

Recording your feelings in a weekly diary: My Feelings Chart
Telling people how you feel in your body: Things I Feel In My Body
Getting ready to speak to your GP about your mental health: GP Information Pack
